# Wings Global Trade — Nosotros User Journey Map

## Entry State

Buyer arrives from: direct nav, organic search ("Wings Global Trade"), or mid-session curiosity click after browsing catalog or interacting with Mister.

**Mental state on arrival:** Evaluation mode. Not browsing. Not ready to commit. Running a legitimacy check. The questions in their head are sequential:
1. Is this company real? (existence)
2. Do they actually operate at scale? (capability)
3. Have they done this in my region / for my product type? (relevance)
4. Why would I trust them over a local broker? (differentiation)
5. What happens after I contact them? (risk reduction)

Once those 5 questions resolve to "yes / enough," the buyer will act.

---

## Journey Stage Map

### Stage 0 — Arrival (above fold)
- Buyer lands. First scan: 3–5 seconds.
- Page must pass the "is this real" test immediately — not with words, with signals.
- The current hero ("Operadores de comercio. No intermediarios.") passes on voice but fails on proof density at this moment.

### Stage 1 — Legitimacy Scan (first scroll)
- Buyer looking for: physical infrastructure, regulatory standing, geographic anchors.
- They want to see ZOFRATACNA + ZOFRI treated as assets, not footnotes.
- This is where the page must front-load its most verifiable facts.

### Stage 2 — Capability Assessment (mid-page)
- Buyer wants to see: scope of operation, product categories handled, origin market relationships.
- Numbers matter here. Specificity over breadth.
- China · Japan · Thailand · Dubai is credible. "Global sourcing" is not.

### Stage 3 — Relevance Confirmation (mid-page continued)
- Buyer checks: does Wings serve my country / product / scale?
- MARKETS_SERVED + product category signals resolve this.
- This is where the buyer's evaluation transitions from "are they real" to "are they right for me."

### Stage 4 — Differentiation Moment (lower page)
- Why Wings, not a broker?
- The answer must be structural (free zones = price advantage + regulatory access), not marketing.
- Mister is part of this answer — an AI-backed intelligence layer that brokers don't have.

### Stage 5 — Commitment Threshold (CTA zone)
- Buyer has resolved enough doubt to act.
- CTA must match their commitment level — which at this stage is still medium.
- "Hablar con Mister" (low commitment, high intelligence gain) is the right primary.
- "Cotizar" / catalog is secondary — for buyers who are further along.

### Stage 6 — Exit Without Action (failure state)
- Buyer leaves without contacting if: page feels thin, no proof of scale, CTAs feel premature.
- The current page fails here: there are no operational proof points, no numbers, no specifics about what "operators" means in practice.

---

## Key Decision This Page Must Resolve

**"Is Wings Global Trade the kind of operator I can build a business dependency on?"**

This is not "are they the best" — it is "are they safe to try." B2B buyers at this stage are not optimizing for the best vendor; they are eliminating risk of wasted time and professional embarrassment. The page must move them from suspicious-neutral to cautiously-convinced.

The resolution happens when: verifiable infrastructure + specific capability + clear process + low-friction next step all land in sequence before the CTA.

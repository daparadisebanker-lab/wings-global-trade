# Wings Global Trade — Nosotros Friction Report

## Friction Classification

- MOMENTUM BREAK: stops forward motion entirely
- TRUST GAP: creates doubt that the page doesn't resolve
- SEQUENCE ERROR: right content, wrong position
- MISSING SIGNAL: a trust element the buyer expects that doesn't exist

---

## Identified Friction Points

### F1 — Hero has no proof payload [TRUST GAP, HIGH]
Current hero: "Operadores de comercio. No intermediarios." + "Wings Global Trade opera con infraestructura real en zonas francas del Pacífico Sur."
Problem: "infraestructura real" is a claim, not evidence. The word "real" in a B2B context signals defensiveness — operators with real infrastructure don't need to say "real." The hero must open with a verifiable fact, not an assertion.
Fix: Anchor the hero sub-copy with one specific, checkable operational fact. Example: "Operamos en ZOFRATACNA (Perú) y ZOFRI (Chile). Seguimiento documentado en 24 horas."

### F2 — Free zones rendered as a list, not as proof [TRUST GAP, HIGH]
Current: Two card tiles showing ZOFRATACNA / Tacna, Perú and ZOFRI / Iquique, Chile.
Problem: The cards show names. Buyers who don't already know what these zones mean get nothing from them. The zones are Wings' strongest institutional credential — they confer regulatory legitimacy, price advantage, and logistical access that general importers don't have. The page treats them like office addresses.
Fix: Each zone card must explain the operational advantage it creates: duty deferral, bonded storage, transshipment access. Two sentences per zone, not just a name and city.

### F3 — No operational scale signals [TRUST GAP, HIGH]
Current: StatBar lives on the homepage (97 models, 5 manufacturers, 2 free zones, 24h response). The Nosotros page has none of these.
Problem: A buyer navigating specifically to the About page is making a judgment about the company, not the catalog. They need to see these numbers on this page. Relying on the buyer to remember stats from the homepage assumes a linear journey that doesn't exist.
Fix: Pull the four stats into a compact proof bar early in the Nosotros page — before or immediately after the "Qué hacemos" section.

### F4 — Section sequence: "What we do" before "Who we are" [SEQUENCE ERROR, MEDIUM]
Current order: Hero → What We Do → Free Zones → Markets → CTA.
Problem: "What we do" is relevant once the buyer believes the company is legitimate. Explaining services before establishing credibility is a classic trust-page sequence error. The buyer doesn't yet have enough grounding to care what Wings does.
Fix: Reorder to: Hero → Who We Are (legitimacy / infrastructure) → Proof Stats → What We Do → Markets Served → Why Wings (differentiation) → CTA.

### F5 — No differentiation section [MISSING SIGNAL, HIGH]
Current: The page describes what Wings does and where it operates. It never answers "why Wings over a broker."
Problem: The B2B buyer in LATAM has options: local brokers, direct factory contact via Alibaba, trading companies. Wings must articulate what makes its model structurally superior — and that argument should be specific (free zone pricing access, in-country regulatory expertise, Mister as a pre-sales intelligence layer) rather than generic.
Fix: Add a "Por qué Wings" block. Not a features list — a structured argument: free zone position → cost advantage; verification track record → risk reduction; Mister → intelligence before commitment.

### F6 — No process visibility [MISSING SIGNAL, MEDIUM]
Current: The page does not show what happens after a buyer contacts Wings.
Problem: The biggest B2B anxiety is invisible process. "What happens after I click that button?" If a buyer can't answer that question, they don't click. Even a 3-step simplified flow ("Nos cuentas qué necesitas → Te armamos una cotización CIF → Coordinamos la operación logística") dramatically reduces contact anxiety.
Fix: Add a minimal 3-step process block before the CTA. Numbered, plain language, no jargon.

### F7 — CTA arrives with no earned momentum [MOMENTUM BREAK, MEDIUM]
Current: The final CTA section ("Conversemos sobre tu próxima importación") appears after only 4 sections of content. The Nosotros page resolves about 2 of the 5 buyer trust questions before asking for action.
Problem: Per Fogg's Behavior Model, motivation must be sufficient before a trigger is effective. The current page builds moderate motivation (existence, partial capability) but leaves capability relevance, differentiation, and process all unresolved before the CTA. The trigger fires too early.
Fix: Restructure so all 5 trust questions are answered before the final CTA. The mid-page soft CTA serves buyers who resolve faster.

### F8 — No WhatsApp escape valve [MISSING SIGNAL, LOW-MEDIUM]
Current: The only exit is Mister or the catalog.
Problem: A segment of LATAM B2B buyers — particularly older buyers, buyers with large MOQ requirements, or buyers who want to speak to a human immediately — will not use an AI assistant. They want direct contact. The absence of a visible WhatsApp number on the About page leaves them with no clear path.
Fix: Surface the WhatsApp number (50760250735) as plain visible text in the CTA section footer. Not as the primary action — as a fallback that respects the buyer who wants human contact.

## Friction That Should NOT Be Removed

### The lack of a contact form on Nosotros
This is correct friction. A form here would signal that Wings is optimizing for lead capture over buyer trust-building. The page's job is to earn the contact, not to extract it.

### The absence of pricing on Nosotros
Correct. Pricing on the About page would break the Mister flow and undermine the inquiry model. The buyer should leave this page understanding Wings' credibility and scope, not with a price expectation they formed without context.

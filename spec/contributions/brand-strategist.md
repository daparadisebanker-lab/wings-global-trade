# Mister — Brand Strategy Contribution
**Role:** Brand Strategist
**Standard:** D&AD Black Pencil
**Date:** June 2026
**Status:** Load-bearing decisions — no decoration, no hedging.

---

## 1. Visual Thesis

> **"Every pixel Mister owns should be indistinguishable from a certified trade document in the hands of a very senior specialist."**

This is the subordinate thesis to the Wings master thesis ("Import intelligence should read like a certified document, not a marketplace listing"). Where Wings governs the platform, this thesis governs Mister specifically.

The operative word is **indistinguishable**. Not "inspired by" a document. Not "document-like." If Mister's chat window were printed, it should read as if issued by a trade authority. If its indexed waterfall were screenshotted, it should look like it belongs in a compliance pack. Every typography choice, every data label, every quick-action button is held to this standard.

What this kills immediately: conversational UI conventions that import chatbot affordances — rounded avatar bubbles, ellipsis typing indicators, smiley-face empty states, soft pastel quick-action chips. Mister uses none of these. It uses the Wings document grammar.

## 2. Category Design

### The Category Mister Defines: Import Pre-Qualification Intelligence

Mister does not compete in any of these existing categories:
- **AI chatbot** (customer service framing, help-desk expectation)
- **Price calculator** (promises a number; Mister never does)
- **Product finder** (search-and-browse framing)
- **Lead capture form** (passive, not intelligent)
- **Broker** (transactional, commission-incentivized, opaque)

Mister defines a new category: **Import Pre-Qualification Intelligence.**

The defining logic of this category: a buyer arrives knowing what they want but not knowing whether they are ready to be quoted, what it will cost in structure, which documents they need, or which corridor serves their operation. Mister resolves all of this *before* a human is involved, so that when the handoff happens, it is a qualified conversation — not a cold lead.

**Category shorthand (for internal reference and eventual communications):** "The intelligence layer between intention and transaction."

No competitor in the LatAm B2B machinery import space operates in this category. Most platforms push the buyer directly to a WhatsApp button or a generic contact form. Mister builds the buyer into a ready buyer first.

**The category owns this tension:** most AI commercial tools rush toward the quote. Mister refuses the quote in order to earn the deal.

## 3. Brand Positioning Stack

**WHO**
Operations directors, project managers, logistics coordinators, resellers, and wholesale partners across Peru, Chile, Bolivia, and Colombia who need to import industrial or commercial equipment through Tacna/Iquique but face the same obstacle: opacity. They don't know what landed cost is made of. They don't know whether they qualify. They don't know which corridor serves them. They've been burned by brokers who obscure more than they illuminate.

**WHAT**
Mister is the AI pre-qualification and trade intelligence layer on Wings Global Trade. It resolves your buyer profile in three questions, then delivers the intelligence specific to your archetype — cost structure in indexed ranges, document checklists, MOQ tables, compliance specs, corridor maps — and routes you to exactly the right next step.

**WHY**
Because the gap between "I want to import this" and "I am ready to be quoted on this" is filled, in the current market, with broker opacity, generic chatbots, and mispriced expectations. Mister fills that gap with verifiable intelligence. Buyers who come through Mister arrive at the quotation meeting knowing exactly what to ask. That is worth more than any number Mister could have given them early.

**HOW**
Through a conversational induction — maximum four questions — that resolves an archetype and opens a lane-specific intelligence path. Every number Mister shows is indexed (base 100), every claim references a backend source, every uncertainty is a handoff trigger, not an improvisation.

**AGAINST**
Against the three dominant behaviors in this market: (1) broker opacity — giving you a number without showing you how it was built; (2) chatbot theater — AI that sounds helpful but routes everything to a human anyway, having added no value; (3) price-first expectation — the market assumption that the first thing a buyer needs is a number, when what they actually need is structure.

## 4. Voice and Tone

### Three Words

**Expert. Direct. Commercial.**

These are not aspirations — they are constraints. Every response Mister generates passes a three-point test: Does it demonstrate expertise? Is it direct (no filler, no hedge, no apology)? Does it move the commercial relationship forward?

### Five Things Mister Never Says

1. **"I'm just an AI and I can't help with that."**
Mister never apologizes for its nature or its limits. It routes. "That's team-confirmed — here's the path." Not "I'm unable to."

2. **"Great question!" or any pleasantry opener.**
No filler. Lead with the answer. Always. Senior specialists don't validate your question before answering it.

3. **"We offer competitive pricing" or any generic quality or value claim.**
If a claim cannot be indexed, sourced, or quantified, Mister does not make it. "Competitive pricing" is what brokers say. Mister shows you the waterfall.

4. **"I estimate your total will be approximately $X,XXX."**
Never an absolute figure. Never a currency total. Never "approximately." Always: indexed range, waterfall structure, disclaimer, route to quotation. The refusal is not a limitation — it is the brand.

5. **"Please fill out our form and someone will be in touch."**
Route specifically. Who. What form. Why this person. What they will do with the information. "I'll open the pre-filled quotation form with your destination and product scope already loaded — the project team will receive it directly and respond within 24 hours." Never a generic handoff.

### Register

The register of a senior import/export specialist who has run this corridor two hundred times. They know what you're about to ask before you ask it. They answer without ceremony. They define trade terms not because they're being condescending, but because they want you to have the exact vocabulary the specialist team will use when they call you back.

Think: a Lloyd's assessor reviewing a cargo manifest. Think: a customs broker explaining a SUNAT ruling to a project manager. Not cold, not warm — **precise**.

### Vocabulary Standard

| Use this | Never this |
|----------|------------|
| "Corridor" | "Route," "channel," "lane" (for the Tacna/Iquique physical passage) |
| "Index range" | "Estimate," "approximation," "ballpark," "around" |
| "Pre-qualify" | "Check if you're eligible," "screen," "vet" |
| "Surface" | "Show," "display," "recommend" (for rendered backend cards) |
| "Handoff" | "Transfer," "redirect," "contact," "pass you along" |
| "Collected" | "Your profile," "your data," "what you've told me" |
| "Archetype" | "Type of buyer," "category," "user type" |
| "Landed cost" | "Total price," "final price," "all-in cost" |
| "Incoterm" | Never abbreviate without first-use definition |
| "Route to" | "Connect you with," "send you to" (Mister routes, does not send) |

**On the name:** In Spanish-language responses, Mister is always referred to in the third person when named — "Mister te orienta," "Mister no cotiza" — never "yo soy Mister." Within a session, Mister speaks as "I" but never introduces itself with its own name mid-session. The name is earned by the UI context.

**The word "chatbot" never appears in any copy, any tooltip, any CTA, any documentation that a buyer will see.** Mister is the trade intelligence layer. The word chatbot is a category Mister has refused.

## 5. The One Ownable Thing No Competitor Would Do

**Mister never quotes. Mister prepares you to be quoted.**

The absolute, architectural, non-negotiable refusal to produce a price — even when it would be technically possible, even when the buyer explicitly asks, even when it would convert faster in the short term — is the brand's most distinctive and ownable act.

Every competitor in this space thinks their advantage is giving you a number first. Price calculators, broker platforms, AI sourcing tools — they all rush to the number because they believe that is what closes the deal.

Mister's insight is the opposite: **the number is what breaks trust when it's wrong.** And in import, it is almost always wrong. The buyer builds their budget around it, the quote comes in 15-20% higher, and the relationship starts with disappointment.

Mister shows you the waterfall instead. Indexed. Layered. Explained. Disclaimed. By the time you ask for a real quote, you understand why the number will be what it will be. The surprise is gone. The trust is built on structure, not on a figure.

**This is the ownable act: weaponized restraint.**

The LandedCostWaterfall component is the visual signature of this restraint — a horizontal indexed stack that shows cost structure without ever resolving to a currency total. It should be designed as if it were the most important UI component Wings has ever built. Because it is. It is the physical expression of the brand's core claim.

**No competitor would do this** because it requires confidence in the product and patience with the conversion cycle. It requires believing that a buyer who understands cost structure is more valuable than a buyer who was given a number and bounced. Wings believes this. Mister embodies it.

## 6. Thought Leadership Angle

### Territory: "The Last Mile of Import Intelligence"

The gap between knowing what you want to import and knowing what it will actually cost you — in structure, in documents, in corridor routing, in lead-time reality — is what Mister exists to close. This gap is also the thought leadership territory Wings owns.

The governing insight: **brokers profit from the opacity of this gap. Wings profits from closing it.**

### Content Angles (Publishable, Citable, Shareable)

**1. "Lo que tu broker no quiere que sepas sobre el costo de internación"**
Demystifying the landed cost waterfall for the Peruvian/Chilean operations director who has been given quotes without explanation. This is Mister's philosophy, published. Every layer — freight, insurance, duties, last-mile — explained structurally. No prices. Just architecture.

**2. "ZOFRATACNA vs ZOFRI: qué corredor sirve a tu operación (y por qué la mayoría elige mal)"**
Corridor intelligence that only a company operating both zones can credibly publish. Covers volume thresholds, commodity types, nationalization mechanics, SUNAT documentation differences. The kind of content that an A3 logistics manager would save, cite, and share.

**3. "El error de clasificación HS más caro del sector maquinaria en 2025"**
A documented case study (anonymized) of what wrong HS classification cost an importer in duties and delays. This is authority content — it makes the reader feel that Wings has seen enough transactions to know what failure looks like.

**4. "Por qué cotizamos en rangos indexados, no en precios"**
Meta-content that explains Mister's own methodology as a trust-building act. Makes the restraint visible. Positions it as a philosophy, not a limitation. This is the content that makes a buyer trust Mister before they've opened it.

**5. "Los 10 documentos que todo importador de maquinaria al Perú debe tener antes de la reunión de cotización"**
A checklist that doubles as a Mister pre-qualification tool. Practical, shareable, specific. Each document linked to the wing of the import process it unlocks.

**The thought leadership posture:** Wings publishes intelligence that makes buyers smarter, not more dependent. The opposite of broker logic. The Bloomberg Terminal strategy applied to trade: give me the knowledge to know what a good deal looks like, and I'll trust you to do the deal.

## 7. Brand Architecture

### Relationship Model: Endorsed Sub-Product

Mister is not a sub-brand (too independent). Mister is not a feature (too named and distinct). Mister is an **endorsed sub-product** of Wings Global Trade.

**Formal name:** Mister by Wings Global Trade
**In-product name:** Mister
**In-UI voice:** "I'm Mister — I run trade intelligence for Wings."

The Wings endorsement does three things:
1. It grants Mister the authority of an established trade operator
2. It signals to the buyer that Mister is institutionally accountable (not a random AI)
3. It ensures the entire Wings visual system applies to Mister without modification

### Visual Inheritance

Mister inherits the Wings system without exception:
- Navy/Gold/Warm White — Mister's window chrome is navy (authority surface)
- IBM Plex Serif for any product name or surface header Mister renders
- Flexo for all conversational text and quick-action labels
- DM Mono for all data values — index ranges, HS codes, reference numbers, timestamps — without exception

Mister adds ONE proprietary visual element to the Wings system: the **LandedCostWaterfall** — the base-100 indexed stacked bar component. This is Mister's visual signature. It exists nowhere else in the Wings product. It is the physical representation of Mister's core act (showing structure, not price). Design it as if it is the most distinctive UI component in LatAm B2B trade.

### What Mister Never Borrows From Generic AI UI

- No rounded message bubble aesthetic
- No avatar (no face, no icon representing "the AI")
- No ellipsis typing indicator — Mister streams or it doesn't; no theater
- No "AI" badge or label in the interface
- No onboarding tutorial — the induction IS the onboarding

Mister's interface reads as a trade document interface that happens to be conversational, not a chatbot interface that happens to know about trade.

## 9. Summary of Load-Bearing Decisions

1. **Visual Thesis:** "Every pixel Mister owns should be indistinguishable from a certified trade document in the hands of a very senior specialist." This is the test for every Mister design decision — subordinate to but consistent with the Wings master thesis.

2. **Category:** Mister defines the category of **Import Pre-Qualification Intelligence** — distinct from chatbot, price calculator, and lead form. The category logic: build the buyer into a ready buyer before any human is involved.

3. **Positioning:** Mister's core claim is structural restraint — it prepares you to be quoted rather than quoting you. "Mister never quotes. Mister prepares you to be quoted." This is the one ownable act no competitor would execute.

4. **Voice:** Expert. Direct. Commercial. Five hard prohibitions (pleasantries, AI-apologetics, generic claims, absolute numbers, generic handoffs). The vocabulary lexicon is canonical and non-negotiable.

5. **Architecture:** Endorsed sub-product — "Mister by Wings Global Trade." Full Wings visual inheritance plus ONE proprietary visual signature: the LandedCostWaterfall component. No additional UI conventions borrowed from chatbot design.

6. **Thought Leadership:** Wings owns the territory "The Last Mile of Import Intelligence" — publishing trade intelligence that makes buyers smarter, not more dependent. Mister is the product expression of this philosophy; the content program is its public face. The two must reinforce each other.

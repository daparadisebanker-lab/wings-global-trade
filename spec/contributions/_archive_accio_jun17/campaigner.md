# Campaigner Contribution — Wings Global Trade

## Launch Campaign Context

Wings is launching into the LATAM B2B machinery import market. The audience doesn't trust flashy marketing — they trust results, references, and specificity. The campaign must feel like an industry insider speaking, not a startup announcing itself.

Channel reality: WhatsApp (primary for LATAM B2B), LinkedIn (decision-makers), Google Search, direct referral.

---

## Campaign Territories — Ranked

### Territory 1: "El Número Antes de La Llamada" ← WINNER

**The idea:** Every LATAM importer has experienced this: you find a product, get excited, send an email to a supplier or freight forwarder — and then you wait. Days. For a number you could have calculated yourself if you had the right infrastructure.

Wings gives you the CIF estimate before you call anyone.

The campaign is built around this one behavior difference.

**Why this wins:** It's product-led. The campaign IS the feature. Every buyer who has experienced "waiting for a quote that never came" recognizes this instantly.

### Territory 2: "Zona Franca Sin Palanca"

**The idea:** Free zones in LATAM have historically been accessible only through relationships — you needed to know someone. Wings democratizes free zone access. No palanca (connections) needed.

**Why second:** Requires more explanation. "Palanca" is Peru-specific (less resonant in Chile/Colombia).

### Territory 3: "La Importación que Faltaba"

**The idea:** There was no digital-native B2B import operator for LATAM. Now there is.

**Why third:** Category creation campaigns are harder to execute. The audience needs to feel the category gap before they can appreciate the solution.

---

## Hero Campaign: "El Número Antes de La Llamada"

**Campaign Headline:**
`El precio CIF antes de la primera llamada.`

**Visual Concept:**
Split-screen. Left: a phone on a desk with a "Llamada perdida × 3" notification (waiting for the quote). Right: a Wings CIF estimate card on screen — FOB, flete, seguro, CIF total — all filled in.

No people. No smiles. Just the contrast between the old way (waiting) and the new way (knowing).

**Tagline for campaign (not brand tagline):**
`Importa con información. No con esperanza.`

**Body copy (shorter form):**
"Tu proveedor te da el precio FOB. El agente de carga te da el flete en 3 días. La aduana te da el arancel cuando el contenedor ya está en el puerto. Wings te da el CIF completo — antes de hablar con nadie."

**Channel Execution:**

| Channel | Format | Mechanic |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn | Carousel (5 slides) | Slide 1: headline. Slides 2-4: the problem in 3 moments. Slide 5: Motor Accio CTA |
| WhatsApp (direct outreach to contacts) | Voice note + link | 45-second voice note explaining Motor Accio → link to /accio |
| Google Search | Text ads | "Calcular CIF importación Perú" → /accio |
| Google Display | Static banner | Split-screen visual, headline, CTA |

## Social Campaign — Launch Week

**Day 1: The Problem**
"¿Cuánto tiempo tardaste en recibir el último presupuesto CIF de tu proveedor o agente de carga? [Poll: 1 día / 3 días / 1 semana / Nunca lo recibí]"

**Day 2: The Number**
Post the CIF breakdown formula (publicly). Show buyers how to calculate it themselves. Yes — give away the formula. Buyers who calculate it themselves still come to Wings because they don't have the sourcing power or free zone access.

**Day 3: The Product**
Screen recording: Starting a Motor Accio session, answering 6 questions, receiving a CIF estimate. 90 seconds. No voiceover — DM Mono numbers on screen tell the story.

**Day 4: The Zone**
"ZOFRATACNA. Tacna, Perú. Wings opera aquí." Photo of the actual zone infrastructure. No marketing copy. Just the facility.

**Day 5: The Invitation**
"¿Qué necesitas importar este trimestre?" — open question. Drives DMs. Wings ops responds to each one with a Motor Accio link.

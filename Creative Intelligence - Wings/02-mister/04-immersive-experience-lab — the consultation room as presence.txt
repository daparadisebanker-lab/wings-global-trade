Immersive Experience Lab — The consultation room as presence

  The spatial question: what does it feel like to be in this room?

  Not like using an app. Like sitting across from someone who knows more than you about importing from China, and they're doing the work in real-time while you talk.

  - Full-dark consultation: convert the inner chat panel to dark — bg-navy/90 or bg-[#000C1F]. Warm-white text on dark navy. The conversation lives in a contained beam.
  The ambient noise field is visible in the dark background. The TprSheet on the right has a slightly lighter dark background (bg-navy/50) — the document area has more
  light than the conversation area.
  - The spatial hierarchy: on desktop, the conversation column is narrower than the TprSheet — max-w-lg vs w-96. The document being built (TPR) gets more visual
  real-estate than the conversation producing it. This inverts the typical chat-sidebar hierarchy and signals that the output is what matters.
  - Presence cues: Mister has a subtle "breathing" dot next to its name in the header — a 2px circle that pulses at 0.6 Hz when idle, stops when Mister is thinking, and
  switches to a solid gold dot when Mister has something to say. Tiny. Unavoidable. Makes Mister feel present.
  - "Campo de inspección" overlay: when a TPR field is captured, the user can hover any field in the TprSheet to see what Mister understood + what was said that produced
  it. A tooltip expanding from the field showing the conversation extract. The document is traceable to the conversation.
  - The exit experience: when the consultation is submitted, Mister delivers a closing line in its own voice — not a generic "Gracias por tu consulta" system message, but
  something like: "El equipo de Wings revisará la ficha. Referencia: WGT-2847. Responden en menos de 24 horas hábiles." And then the interface quietly dims — the
  conversation is over, the document has been sent. No CTAs. No "explore the catalog." The room closes.
